### [Buffett
                                      has an interesting theory about
                                      why self-driving cars will hurt
                                      the insurance industry](http://www.cnbc.com/2017/02/27/buffett-self-driving-cars-will-hurt-the-insurance-industry.html)

E. Gurdus, Feb 27, "The
                                    self-driving car business could
                                    become a major threat to insurance
                                    companies when the technology hits
                                    the market, billionaire investor
                                    Warren Buffett told CNBC's "Squawk
                                    Box" on Monday.

                                    If autonomous vehicles prove to be
                                    safer than regular cars, insurance
                                    costs will plummet, and by the time
                                    roads are filled with self-driving
                                    cars insurers like Geico will have
                                    taken a serious hit, Buffett said...

"If I had to take the over and
                                    under [bet] ten years from now on
                                    whether 10 percent of the cars on
                                    the road would be self-driving, I
                                    would take the under, but I could
                                    very easily be wrong," he said...."
                                    [Read
                                      more](http://www.cnbc.com/2017/02/27/buffett-self-driving-cars-will-hurt-the-insurance-industry.html)Hmmm...Really
                                        shouldn't go against Buffet;
                                        however, he's going to be
                                        smiling all the way to the
                                        bank.  I just don't see how the
                                        premise implies Geico takes a
                                        serious hit.  I tell everyone
                                        that I don't understand
                                        insurance.  I guess I just don't
                                        understand insurance.  :-(

I suspect
                                        that by cars he means
                                        cars + light trucks for which
                                        there are about 250M currently
                                        registered in the US with 38%
                                        being greater than 10 years
                                        old.  Assuming these basic
                                        numbers remain roughly constant:
                                        of the 155M vehicles sold in the
                                        next 10 years, 25M or 16% would
                                        need to be 'Self-driving'.
                                        Since we are starting from a
                                        zero base with zero production,
                                        we are going to need to be
                                        upwards of a 30% adoption rate
                                        in the 10th year in order to
                                        have populated 16% of the fleet
                                        through that year.  So, I agree
                                        with Warren wrt 'Self-driving'":  "I would take
                                        the under, but I could very
                                        easily be wrong"  Wrt
                                        'Safe-driving, I would
                                        take the over, because the early
                                        numbers are attainable,
                                        especially if Insurance comes on
                                        board.  Wrt 'Diverless':
                                        No way unless they are
                                        manufactured by a
                                        non-traditional entity that is
                                        totally disruptive in years 8,
                                        9 and 10.   Alain
